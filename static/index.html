Community-Defined Fairness (CDF v1.0)

Who decides what “fair” means?

Every AI system uses a fairness threshold — a number that determines when the system is “fair enough.” Today, that number comes from a federal regulation written in 1978 or a vendor’s legal team.

CDF v1.0 replaces that number with one that comes from the community. Documented, SHA-256 hash-chained, and tamper-evident — set by the people the AI system actually affects.

  • Communities define their own fairness threshold (θ)
  • Cryptographic Provenance Ledger — every decision hash-chained
  • Q2Q Translation Matrix — qualitative input → quantitative parameters
  • Staleness tracking — standards expire and get reviewed
  • Priority groups identified by the community, not assumed
