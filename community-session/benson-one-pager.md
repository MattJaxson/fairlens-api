# District 3 AI Fairness Rating Session
## One-Page Briefing for Councilman Benson

---

### What This Is

A 90-minute session where 10-15 District 3 residents set the fairness
standard for AI systems operating in their community — starting with Emily.

### The Problem

Every AI system uses a fairness threshold — a number that determines when
the system is "fair enough." Right now that number comes from:

- A federal regulation written in 1978
- The technology vendor's lawyers
- Or nobody at all

No one asks the community. District 3 residents have no formal voice in
whether Emily is serving them fairly.

### What Happens at the Session

1. **Residents share experiences** with automated systems (10 min)
2. **Residents evaluate 4 scenarios** — real numbers showing what "legal"
   means vs what "fair" means in their neighborhood (40 min)
3. **Residents set a fairness threshold** — the minimum acceptable
   performance for all demographic groups in District 3 (20 min)
4. **The standard is documented and signed** (20 min)

### What You Walk Out With

A signed **District 3 Community Fairness Standard** — a one-page document
stating the threshold your constituents set, the groups they prioritized,
and full provenance (who participated, when, how consensus was reached).

This document:
- Can be presented at city council
- Gives you documented grounds to hold the Emily vendor accountable
- Positions District 3 as the first community in America to formally
  govern a municipal AI system through direct resident input

### Why This Matters for District 3

- **The UMich survey found** Detroiters are skeptical about AI fairness.
  This directly addresses that skepticism with action, not promises.
- **Emily processes calls from a majority-Black district.** AI speech
  recognition has documented failures with Black American English. This
  ensures Emily is tested against your constituents' actual standard.
- **The data center moratorium** showed you can slow down tech. This
  shows you can also govern it. Both messages together are coherent
  and powerful.

### What This Costs

- One evening
- A room that seats 20
- 10-15 residents you'd invite to any community meeting

I handle everything else: facilitation, documentation, the formal
standard, and a follow-up report.

### The Commitment

This isn't one-and-done. I'll return in 6 months with a report showing
how Emily performed against the standard your residents set. If it meets
the bar — you have mandate to expand. If it doesn't — you have documented
grounds to demand fixes.
