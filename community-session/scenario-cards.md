# District 3 AI Fairness Rating Session
## Scenario Cards

*Read each scenario aloud. After each one, ask the room: "Is this fair?"*

---

## Scenario 1: Equal Performance

> The city's AI system, Emily, correctly handles service calls from
> White residents **80% of the time** and Black residents **80% of
> the time**.
>
> Both groups get the same quality of service.

**Discussion question:** *Is this fair?*

---

## Scenario 2: The Gap

> Emily correctly handles service calls from White residents **80%
> of the time** but only handles Black residents' calls correctly
> **55% of the time**.
>
> That means nearly half of Black residents' calls are misrouted,
> misunderstood, or dropped.

**Discussion question:** *Is this acceptable? Even temporarily?*

---

## Scenario 3: What the Law Says Is "Good Enough"

> The federal government uses something called the "four-fifths rule."
> It says a system is legally compliant as long as the disadvantaged
> group gets at least **80% of the rate** the advantaged group gets.
>
> In real numbers: if White residents' calls are handled correctly
> 80% of the time, the legal minimum for Black residents is only
> **64%**. That means more than 1 in 3 calls from Black residents
> could fail — and it would still be considered legal.

**Discussion question:** *Does that match what you consider fair
for District 3?*

---

## Scenario 4: Your Standard

> You get to set the number.
>
> If White residents' calls are handled correctly 80% of the time,
> **what is the minimum percentage for Black residents that you
> would consider acceptable in District 3?**

**This is your community's standard. There is no wrong answer.**

Write your number on your participant form (Q2).

---

*After everyone has written their number, we'll discuss as a group
and agree on a District 3 standard.*
